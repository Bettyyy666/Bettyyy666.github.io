Chapter 01 — Introduction

About Me

I'm Rui (Betty) Zhou, a Computer Science M.S. candidate at Brown University with a 4.0 GPA, working in the Brown Visual Computing Group. My research sits at the intersection of computer vision, generative models, and 3D understanding.

I co-authored a WACV 2026 paper on controllable human-scene interaction video generation and currently lead a program synthesis project bridging sewing pattern design and body measurement grading. Before Brown, I studied at UC Davis (B.S. in CS, minor in Managerial Economics) and took visiting coursework at Harvard.

My industry experience spans building production ML pipelines at Style3D, where I worked on fabric try-on features adopted by 60+ enterprise clients, and architecting RAG data pipelines at UC Davis's Data Visualization Lab. I care deeply about making complex systems feel intuitive and beautiful.

Research
